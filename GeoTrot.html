GeoTrot

A way to be more accurate when you're off the beaten path

About

Geologists in the field aren't necessarily receiving the most accurate longitude and latitude to find their "source materials". They require more precise and reliable mapping for areas that are dense with natural resources (minerals, forests, water, etc.). According to the University of Pittsburgh Department of Geology website, their students are embarking on areas of research that include, but are not limited to "isotope geochemistry, applied geophysics and tectonics, paleoclimatology, planetary science, remote sensing and GIS, volcanology, and human-environmental interactions"(Geology.pitt.edu 2017). So, obviously, this is more than just kicking up dirt. We want to change that.

Experience. Time. Computers messing up. You know, things like that.

By crowdsourcing the exploits of geologists in academia, Google street view could become more accurate in terms of source materials. Instead of having wonderful visuals of sweeping landscapes, this application would allow images to enrich the overall mapping of Google by incorporating specific plot points for a given landscape outside the metropolitan areas.

As an initial first phase measure this application can be utilized, assessed, and developed further by students working in the field. Future development or expansion may include, but not be limited to public use for nature and/or hiking enthusiast, etc.
